initial research and setup for the project.

we are going to create a plugin for Obsidian which turns its editor into a true outliner experience. by true outliner I mean the functionality and UX similar to 

* Logseq (opensource, almost perfect, but stuck for years in a major rewrite)
* outl (new, opensource, similar to Logseq, very promising)
* Tana Outliner (recently split up into a separate app)
* Workflowy
* Roam research (well-known)
* Orca note (mentions bi-directional outlining, [https://orca-studio.com/orcanote](https://orca-studio.com/orcanote/))
* see the general info: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outliner
* do your own research for more concrete details about the UI, the functionality and the mechanics of a good outliner app.

---

after doing the research and getting a good grasp of what the general idea is, let's look at the existing Obsidian plugins:

* https://community.obsidian.md/plugins/obsidian-outliner
   * a good improvement to the default Obsidian experience, improved the writing with nested lists a lot, 
   * but it's still a flat text under the hood, with some cursor tricks bolted on top
      * selecting nodes reveals that very clearly: you can't select node-per-node, only the raw text behind, together with uneven indentation, etc.
         * a clumsy select and delete will leave a broken outline tree with messed up structure and indentation
      * it's still possible to just add a newline and start writing normal flat markdown, next to a list/outline
   * it doesn't "enforce" the structure in any way
   * https://community.obsidian.md/plugins/bullet
      * a fork of the outliner plugin
* https://github.com/vslinko/obsidian-zoom
   * common outline feature, to zoom into a subtree
   * not essential for me, but a nice to have
* https://community.obsidian.md/plugins/draggable-list-items
   * this functionality is probably included in the outliner plugin
* bi-directionality of outline: backlinks view can benefit a lot from relying on the structure of the quoting documents (this is really good in Logseq)
   * https://community.obsidian.md/plugins/better-search-views (not outliner-specific, but tries to improve the structure display in the search/backilns views)
   * https://community.obsidian.md/plugins/influx (a direct attempt, but in a stale/abandoned state)
   * https://community.obsidian.md/plugins/coalesce (a newer attempt at better backlinks display)

it's important not to mix up the Outliner concept with the "sidebar outline" in Obsidian (or any other editor) which shows a TOC/minimam structure of a document. they are related in a way, but we are focusing on the document's structure itself and the editing experience, not an extra UI on a side.
