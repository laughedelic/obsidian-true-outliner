great. this looks pretty good in all cases except one: nested lists (no surprise).

* first, it messes with the built-in indentation guides functionality. when it's enabled, lists sometimes get double guides.
   * the built-in ones are nice because they go from one bullet marker to the next, precisely.
   * our guides at slightly off, they are sort of applied to lists as blocks, not really doing their job of guiding the indentation within the lists.
* second, even when the built-in guides are disabled, the new guides are off and are spaced unevenly. it seems like the deeper levels of a list are indented more that the first level is relative to its parent paragraph, so the guides make that unevenness more obvious.

I know we are not messing with the whole internal list spacing rebalancing. but our current case just doesn't really work with the lists. as an option, I would consider not adding guides for the nested lists and keeping the built-in guides. unless you have a plan for fixing it.
